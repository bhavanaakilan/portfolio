About

A strategist trained in how the brain makes decisions,
applied to how organisations, markets, and founders do the same.

My academic foundation is in cognitive neuroscience and decision science at the University of Toronto Scarborough, where I graduated with an Honours BSc with Double Minors in Psychology and Health Humanities. I was awarded the UofT International Scholar Award ($107,500) and the Laidlaw Leadership and Research Scholarship ($12,500), and was selected as one of 20 Dean's Circle Fellows from over 10,000 undergraduates. My published research applies deep learning to EEG-based Alzheimer's diagnosis, conducted as part of the Laidlaw Scholars Programme at SASTRA Deemed University in India.

My career spans disciplines by design. I have worked as a clinical research assistant conducting psychiatric assessments under Dr. Michael Best at the TIP Lab; as a public health research analyst synthesising evidence across 1,000-plus academic sources at OISE; and as a luxury brand strategist managing 10 concurrent accounts across Southeast Asia for clients including APM Monaco, Poh Heng, and Abel Richard. I currently advise early-stage digital health companies at StrategyRx and conduct quality analytics inside a stealth-stage spatial AI startup backed by Andreessen Horowitz.

In parallel, I co-established a women's mental health collective in Murang'a County, Kenya, through the KULE Foundation International, and founded SIINAI (Society for Innovation at the Intersection of Natural and Artificial Intelligence) at UofT, growing it from zero to 200-plus members in under three months. I am currently building 4-8, a consumer neurotechnology product that makes prefrontal cortical activity measurable in real time via frontal theta EEG oscillations.

I am seeking roles in venture capital, strategy consulting, and senior business development where rigorous analytical thinking and cross-domain pattern recognition create compounding strategic advantage.
